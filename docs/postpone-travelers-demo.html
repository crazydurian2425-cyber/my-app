Why your requirement is safe: travel dates live in the travelers table (+ the meta JSON); planner deadlines live in set_assignments. They're separate, so postponing travelers cannot change the planners' deadlines.
Why it's NOT a one‑line change (the risk): the travel date is stored in three places that must all move together — the arrival_date column and two fields inside the meta JSON (trip_start_date, unit_departure_date). If you shift only arrival_date and miss the meta dates, the displayed date and the itinerary's internal date will disagree for some plans. Also: every shifted plan's transport times get recomputed for the new (future) date → some Google re‑fetches. It's a bulk write to every traveler, so: preview with a SELECT + back up first, and use a fixed day‑count (e.g. 137 days) rather than "4.5 months" so it's cleanly reversible.
