PART 1 · YOU Move the domain into Cloudflare · ~15 min + waiting time

Where dash.cloudflare.com → log in → on the home page press ＋ Add a domain (sometimes shown as "Add site").
Type your domain (just the name, e.g. mycompany.com — no https, no www) → Continue.
Choose the Free plan (bottom of the list) → Continue. Cloudflare scans existing DNS — just press Continue again on the records page.
Cloudflare now shows 2 nameservers, like:
alba.ns.cloudflare.com and rudy.ns.cloudflare.com (yours will differ).
Copy both into your notepad. Keep this tab open.
Where New tab → namesilo.com → log in → top-right menu Manage My Domains.
Find your domain in the list → tick its checkbox → in the icons/actions row press Change Nameservers (globe icon).
You'll see NameSilo's defaults (like NS1.DNSOWL.COM, NS2…, NS3…). Delete/clear ALL of them. Into the first two boxes paste the 2 Cloudflare nameservers from your notepad → Submit / Change Nameservers.
Back in the Cloudflare tab press Continue / Check nameservers now. Now wait — usually 10–30 minutes, sometimes up to 24 h. Cloudflare emails you when the domain shows ✅ Active on your dashboard home. Do not continue to Part 2 until it says Active.

PART 2 · YOU Attach the domain to your site · 2 min

Where dash.cloudflare.com → left menu Workers & Pages → click your app my-app.
Settings tab → Domains & Routes → ＋ Add → choose Custom domain → type your domain (e.g. mycompany.com) → Add domain.
Optional but recommended: press ＋ Add again → Custom domain → www.mycompany.com (so the www version works too).
Test: wait ~5 min, then open https://your-domain in the browser. You should see your current platform login page (still Journey Junction branding — that's correct for now; the new branding comes in Part 5). Your old domain keeps working, nothing changed there.

PART 3 · YOU Let Resend send email as domain 2 · 10 min + short wait

Where resend.com → log in (same account as now) → left menu Domains → ＋ Add Domain → type your domain → Add.
Resend now shows a small table of DNS records (TXT and CNAME/MX types, names like resend._domainkey). Keep this tab open.
Where Other tab: dash.cloudflare.com → click your new domain → left menu DNS → Records → ＋ Add record. Copy each Resend record over: same Type, same Name, same Value. ⚠️ If a record shows an orange cloud 🟠, click it to make it gray ("DNS only") — email records must not be proxied.
Back in the Resend tab → press Verify DNS Records. Wait until the domain shows ✅ Verified (minutes usually). From this moment Resend is allowed to send from anything@your-domain.

PART 4 · YOU Google key — allow the new domain · 5 min

Where console.cloud.google.com → top-left ☰ menu → APIs & Services → Credentials.
Under "API Keys" click your key (the one the platform uses) → under Application restrictions / Website restrictions press ADD → enter https://your-domain/* → and once more for https://www.your-domain/* → Save.
Why: without this, on domain 2 the place search and routes silently fail (empty suggestions). Takes ~5 min to take effect after saving.
